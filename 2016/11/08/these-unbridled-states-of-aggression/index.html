These unbridled states of aggression

2016/11/08

Tomorrow, I won’t be threatening people whose political views I oppose with violence or jail. I won’t be saying the election is rigged. I won’t be speculating about the next civil war while celebrating with friends and neighbors in a house of worship. I won’t suppress the rights of other people to exercise their right to vote. And, I won’t wear t-shirts threatening to lynch journalists.

There are countries where political opponents kill or jail one another, where elections are rigged, where civil wars rage and innocents die, where the right to vote is absent, and where journalists perish seeking out truth.

That is not my country.

Tomorrow, I will be working a very long day to ensure that voting in my precinct is orderly, fair, and safe, that however bitterly my neighbors and I fight about ideas we do so peacefully and with respect for our common humanity, and that every person who is registered to vote gets the full opportunity to exercise that right.

That’s the country I fight for every single day, and I am not alone.
