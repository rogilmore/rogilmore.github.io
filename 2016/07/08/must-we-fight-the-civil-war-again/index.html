Must we fight the Civil War again?

2016/07/08

Must we fight the Civil War again?

That is the fundamental question that this hour of history poses for us to answer.

That is the question raised by the continued popularity of the Confederate flag and the reverence shown to it.

That is the question raised when the people’s armed representatives, officers of the peace, kill brown people again and again and again and again without punishment.

That is the question when neighbors, friends, and loved ones are slaughtered merely because they love differently.

That is the question when we damn an entire religion for the actions of a tiny fraction of its adherents.

Lincoln said “A house divided against itself cannot stand.”

Our house is deeply divided. On that we must all surely agree.

How long can it stand?

Surveying the carnage at Gettysburg, Lincoln observed that “Now, we are engaged in a great civil war testing whether that Nation, or any Nation, so conceived and so dedicated can long endure.”

Our streets are now battlefields.

How long can a Nation dedicated to the proposition that all men and women are created equal endure battle after battle after battle that lays bare the untruth of that aspiration?

Must we fight a Civil War again?

Let there be no misunderstanding. I wish no war.

But, know this: I will fight without rest for a Nation that treats all men and women equally and justly, for a Nation that values the content of a person’s character more than the color of her skin, for a Nation that values peace and life over war and death, for a Nation governed by the rule of law not ruthless lawlessness.

This is what making America great, now, again, or ever, must surely mean.

Let us pledge our lives, fortunes, and sacred honor to this just cause, and let us pledge to do so in peace.
